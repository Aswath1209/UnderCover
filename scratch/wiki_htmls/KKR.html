The 2026 edition was the 19th season for the Indian Premier League franchise Kolkata Knight Riders. They were one of the ten teams competing in the 2026 Indian Premier League. The team finished in eighth place in the previous season's league stage.[1]

The team had a disastrous start to the season, losing five of their first six matches, with one match against the Kings ending in a no-result due to rain. They registered their first win of the season against the Royals on 19 April 2026. They made a strong comeback, winning four consecutive matches before losing to the Royal Challengers on 13 May 2026. The team was eliminated from playoff contention after Royals won their last league match on 24 May 2026. The team lost their last league fixture against the Capitals on the same day and finished 7th in the season.

Angkrish Raghuvanshi was the team's best batter, scoring 422 runs, while Kartik Tyagi was the team's best bowler, clinching 18 wickets in the season. Sunil Narine was also the team's Most Valuable Player (MVP) with 268.5 points. The team finished third in the Fairplay Award list, with an average of 9.86 points.

Pre-season

[edit]
Further information: List of 2026 Indian Premier League personnel changes

Kolkata Knight Riders are a franchise cricket team based in Kolkata, India, which has been playing in the Indian Premier League since its inaugural season. The team has been the third-most successful in the tournament, having won three titles.[2] They were one of the ten teams that competed in the 2026 edition from 28 March to 31 May 2026.[3] They were led by Ajinkya Rahane and coached by Abhishek Nayar.[4] Rinku Singh served as the vice-captain of the team.[5] The team played all its home matches at the Eden Gardens, Kolkata.

Player retention and releases

[edit]

The retentions were announced on 15 November 2025.[6] Kolkata Knight Riders retained a total of 12 players.[7] Big names such as Andre Russell, Venkatesh Iyer, Quinton de Kock, along with eight other players, were released before the auction.[8] During the trade window period, Indian spinner Mayank Markande was traded from Kolkata Knight Riders to Mumbai Indians.[9]

Retirement

[edit]

In the midst of confusion over release and achieving a high auction price, on 30 November 2025, Andre Russell declared his retirement from the Indian Premier League, concluding his 12-year career with the Kolkata Knight Riders franchise.[12] The team later decided to retire Russell's jersey number 12 to honor his contributions during his IPL career.[13]

Auction summary

[edit]

The mini-auction took place on 16 December 2025 in Abu Dhabi, United Arab Emirates.[14] Kolkata entered with the highest purse in the 2026 auction - ₹64.30 crore (US$6.7 million), with 13 slots to fill (including 6 overseas slots).[15] They bought Australian all-rounder Cameron Green for a whopping ₹25.20 crore (US$2.6 million), their most expensive buy in IPL history. Green also clinched the record for the league's most expensive foreign player, surpassing Mitchell Starc's record of ₹24.75 crore (US$2.6 million).[16] They also bought Sri lankan pacer Matheesha Pathirana for a staggering price of ₹18.00 crore (US$1.9 million).[17] The team filled all its 13 remaining slots with ₹45 lakh (US$47,000) left.[18]

Administration and support staff

[edit]

Abhishek Nayar took over as the team's head coach after Chandrakant Pandit's three-year contract ended.[29] Shane Watson replaced Ottis Gibson as the team's assistant coach.[30] Tim Southee replaced Bharat Arun and Carl Crowe as the bowling coach.[31] Andre Russell was appointed as the power coach of the team, a unique role in franchise cricket.[32] Dishant Yagnik has been appointed as the team's fielding coach.[33] Manvinder Bisla and Biju George have joined the team as the new heads of scouting.[34]

Marketing

[edit]

Following the departure of Dream11, which was primarily due to the implementation of the Promotion and Regulation of Online Gaming Act, 2025, Kolkata Knight Riders announced Vida EV, a subsidiary of Hero MotoCorp, as their new title partner for the next three years.[63] RR Kabel continues as the principal partner for the 2026 season as part of their multi-year partnership agreement.[64] In February 2026, the team organized a unique box-cricket tournament featuring only the passionate fan clubs of the Kolkata Knight Riders. They invited popular local influencers to promote the tournament to a larger audience.[65] In March 2026, the team revealed its new jersey, themed around the Lines of Legacy.[66] The team has partnered with Krafton India to deliver exciting franchise-themed in-game content in the latest update of Battlegrounds Mobile India.[67] The team held the Knights Unplugged 3.0, an exclusive, pre-season event which connect players, partners and the fans on 24 March.[68]

Controversies

[edit]

Exclusion of Mustafizur Rahman

[edit]
Further information: 2026 Indian Premier League § Exclusion of Mustafizur Rahman

Bangladeshi fast bowler Mustafizur Rahman was bought by Kolkata for ₹9.2 crore (US$960,000) in the auction.[69] Following the 2025 Bangladesh anti-Hindu violence and lynching of Dipu Chandra Das in December 2025 led to calls for exclusion of Bangladeshi players from IPL.[70] In January 2026, the BCCI requested that the franchise release Rahman from the squad and permitted them to select a replacement player.[71] In March 2026, the team filled the empty spot by adding Zimbabwean pacer Blessing Muzarabani in the squad.[72]

Dwayne Bravo's criticisms

[edit]

The team held an official press conference ahead of the season on March 20, which included the captain Ajinkya Rahane, head coach Abhishek Nayar, assistant coach Shane Watson, and mentor Dwayne Bravo.[73] Before the meeting began, Bravo was overheard speaking to Watson and Nayar, saying, “When these guys called me, I had two women at my home.” Rahane then pointed out to Bravo that the microphones were on, leaving Bravo visibly embarrassed.[74] Two days later, Bravo faced criticism for attending the Chennai Super Kings event instead of helping the Knight Riders team prepare for the upcoming season.[75]
