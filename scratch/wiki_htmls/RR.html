The 2026 Rajasthan Royals season is the 17th season of the Rajasthan Royals franchise in the Indian Premier League (IPL). The season is part of the 2026 Indian Premier League, the 19th edition of the tournament, played between March and May 2026.[1] The franchise entered the season under new leadership, with Riyan Parag appointed as captain following the trade of long-time skipper Sanju Samson to the Chennai Super Kings.[2]

Kumar Sangakkara, the former Sri Lankan captain who had served as the Royals' Director of Cricket since 2021, was reappointed as head coach in November 2025 following Rahul Dravid's departure from the role.[3] The coaching change coincided with a significant off-field development: in May 2026, a consortium led by the Lakshmi Mittal family and Adar Poonawalla, CEO of the Serum Institute of India, reached a definitive agreement to acquire the franchise in a deal valued at approximately US$1.65 billion. Under the new ownership structure, the Mittal family holds approximately 75 per cent, Poonawalla holds 18 per cent, and founding owner Manoj Badale retains a 7 per cent stake.[4]

In the league stage, the Royals finished fourth in the points table with eight wins and six defeats from 14 matches, accumulating 16 points and a net run rate of +0.189.[5] In the Eliminator on 27 May 2026 at the New International Cricket Stadium in New Chandigarh, the Royals defeated the Sunrisers Hyderabad by 47 runs, aided by 97 off 29 balls from Vaibhav Suryavanshi, while Jofra Archer claimed three wickets.[6]

The Royals lost to Gujarat Titans in the Qualifier 2 by 7 wickets and 8 balls left which ended their 2026 season. [7]

Pre-season

[edit]

Trades

[edit]

In a significant restructuring of the squad ahead of the 2026 season, the Rajasthan Royals executed two major trades. In the headline deal, long-time captain Sanju Samson was traded to the Chennai Super Kings in exchange for all-rounder Ravindra Jadeja and pace-bowling all-rounder Sam Curran.[2] Additionally, Nitish Rana was traded to the Delhi Capitals in exchange for wicket-keeper batter Donovan Ferreira.[8]

Player retention

[edit]

On 15 November 2025, the franchise announced its retention list of 13 players ahead of the mini-auction.[9]

Auction

[edit]

The Rajasthan Royals entered the auction with a purse of ₹16.05 crore and nine available slots. Leg-spinner Ravi Bishnoi was the franchise's most expensive acquisition at ₹7.20 crore.[10]
